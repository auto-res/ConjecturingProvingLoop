To prove that the intersection of any two alpha-open sets is alpha-open, we begin with the definition of an alpha-open set in a topological space. 

Let \( X \) be a topological space and \( U \subseteq X \).

A set \( U \) is said to be alpha-open if:
\[ U \subseteq \text{int}(\overline{\text{int}(U)}) \]
where \(\text{int}(U)\) denotes the interior of \( U \) and \(\overline{A}\) denotes the closure of a set \( A \).

Now, consider two alpha-open sets \( U \) and \( V \) in \( X \). By definition, we have:
\[ U \subseteq \text{int}(\overline{\text{int}(U)}) \]
\[ V \subseteq \text{int}(\overline{\text{int}(V)}) \]

We need to show that \( U \cap V \) is alpha-open, i.e., that:
\[ U \cap V \subseteq \text{int}(\overline{\text{int}(U \cap V)}) \]

First, consider the interior of the intersection:
\[ \text{int}(U \cap V) \]

By a property of interiors, we know:
\[ \text{int}(U \cap V) \subseteq \text{int}(U) \cap \text{int}(V) \]

Taking closures of both sides:
\[ \overline{\text{int}(U \cap V)} \subseteq \overline{\text{int}(U) \cap \text{int}(V)} \]

Using the closure of intersection property, we have:
\[ \overline{\text{int}(U) \cap \text{int}(V)} \subseteq \overline{\text{int}(U)} \cap \overline{\text{int}(V)} \]

Consider the interiors:
\[ \text{int}(\overline{\text{int}(U \cap V)}) \subseteq \text{int}(\overline{\text{int}(U)} \cap \overline{\text{int}(V)}) \]

By another property of interiors:
\[ \text{int}(\overline{\text{int}(U)} \cap \overline{\text{int}(V)}) \subseteq \text{int}(\overline{\text{int}(U)}) \cap \text{int}(\overline{\text{int}(V)}) \]

Given the alpha-openness of \( U \) and \( V \), it follows:
\[ U \cap V \subseteq U \subseteq \text{int}(\overline{\text{int}(U)}) \]
\[ U \cap V \subseteq V \subseteq \text{int}(\overline{\text{int}(V)}) \]

Thus:
\[ U \cap V \subseteq \text{int}(\overline{\text{int}(U)}) \cap \text{int}(\overline{\text{int}(V)}) \]

Finally:
\[ U \cap V \subseteq \text{int}(\overline{\text{int}(U \cap V)}) \]

Therefore, the intersection of two alpha-open sets \( U \) and \( V \) is alpha-open. 

Thus, the theorem is true.
